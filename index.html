About me

Clarity from complexity. Vision into action.

My superpower is asking the right questions until alignment emerges. I dive deep into the problems businesses face, understand the needs of internal and external users, then bring clear vision and actionable requirements to the team for execution and iteration.

At Alaska Airlines, I lead the international travel experience product portfolio—a platform spanning iOS, Android, and Web that integrates AI-powered document verification, passport capture, and modernized check-in for millions of guests across two airline brands. Before that, I built digital aviation SaaS at Boeing/Jeppesen, launched Spectrum Mobile at Charter Communications through Slalom, and drove a 17% enrollment increase at Aetna through digital redesign.

“I thrive on ownership, bringing clarity to ambiguity, and creating environments where talented people can do their best work.”

I immigrated to the United States as a teenager, building a career with determination and resilience. My multicultural background—I speak English, Hindi, and Tamil fluently—enriches how I think about user experience, accessibility, and inclusive product design. I've traveled to 34 countries and counting.

Problems I've solved.

Digital Document Verification (DocV)
Alaska Airlines & Hawaiian Airlines · iOS, Android, Web
International travelers were forced to call in or visit the airport to verify travel documents—creating friction, increasing operational costs, and driving poor NPS. I led the 0-to-1 product initiative to build a self-service document verification experience, integrating Jumio's identity verification API with IATA's Timatic regulatory database. Scoped all edge cases including 207 passport nationalities, minors, traveling with pets, and group reservations. Designed a phased rollout via Optimizely across pilot routes, with comprehensive GTM, RACI, and success metrics.
